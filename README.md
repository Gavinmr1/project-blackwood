# 🌲 Project Blackwood

> *A cooperative first-person park ranger simulation where players restore and protect a living national park while uncovering increasingly supernatural mysteries hidden within its wilderness.*

---

## Vision

Project Blackwood is a multiplayer park ranger simulation focused on teamwork, exploration, and environmental stewardship.

Players take on the role of newly hired park rangers responsible for maintaining a vast national park. Daily responsibilities include repairing trails, extinguishing wildfires, rescuing lost hikers, managing wildlife, responding to emergencies, and expanding park facilities.

As players spend more time in the park, they begin discovering evidence that something far older—and stranger—inhabits the wilderness.

The supernatural isn't the focus of the game.

The park is.

---

# Core Pillars

## 🌲 You Are a Park Ranger First

Project Blackwood is built around the experience of being a park ranger.

Players should enjoy:

* Maintaining trails
* Protecting wildlife
* Assisting visitors
* Fighting fires
* Performing search and rescue
* Restoring the park

Even without supernatural encounters, the game should be enjoyable.

---

## 🍃 The Park Is Alive

The park exists independently of the player.

Systems include:

* Dynamic weather
* Wildlife ecosystems
* Visitor behavior
* Forest fires
* Seasonal changes
* Environmental hazards
* Day/Night cycle

The world should continue changing even if the player does nothing.

---

## 👣 Mystery Creates Stories

The unexplained should remain unexplained.

Cryptids are not traditional enemies.

Players discover clues through:

* Ranger journals
* Environmental storytelling
* Strange footprints
* Missing campers
* Radio interference
* Unusual wildlife behavior

Players should leave each session with stories instead of answers.

---

## 🤝 Teamwork Solves Problems

Designed for 1–4 players.

Every player should naturally develop a role.

Examples include:

* Maintenance Ranger
* Wildlife Specialist
* Fire Ranger
* Search & Rescue
* Communications Officer

Cooperation should always feel rewarding.

---

## 🏕️ Restore the Park

The park grows alongside the players.

Every completed task contributes toward restoring and expanding the national park.

Progress is visible through:

* New facilities
* Improved infrastructure
* Expanded trails
* Better equipment
* Additional ranger stations
* Increased visitor activity

Players are building something worth protecting.

---

# Gameplay Loop

1. Receive assignments.
2. Prepare equipment.
3. Patrol the park.
4. Complete maintenance and emergency tasks.
5. Respond to unexpected events.
6. Return to the ranger station.
7. Earn funding.
8. Upgrade equipment and facilities.
9. Repeat.

The loop should remain satisfying regardless of whether supernatural events occur.

---

# Story Premise

The player replaces a veteran ranger who disappeared after years of documenting impossible events throughout the park.

Officially, he retired.

Unofficially...

Nobody knows what happened.

Throughout the game, players uncover his journals hidden across the wilderness.

These journals serve two purposes:

* Slowly reveal the history of the park.
* Provide practical knowledge for surviving unexplained phenomena.

Players eventually realize the previous ranger wasn't imagining things.

---

# Design Philosophy

Project Blackwood aims to create memorable experiences through emergent gameplay rather than scripted events.

The game should encourage moments like:

> "Did anyone else hear that?"

instead of

> "Time to fight the boss."

The wilderness should feel peaceful, beautiful, and occasionally terrifying.

# Development Philosophy

Whenever making a design decision, ask one question:

> **Does this improve the experience of being a park ranger in a mysterious national park?**

If the answer is no, it probably doesn't belong in Project Blackwood.
