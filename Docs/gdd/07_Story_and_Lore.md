# Story and Lore

## Overview

Project Blackwood takes place in a remote wilderness park with a long history of unexplained events.

Players are assigned to replace a veteran ranger who disappeared after documenting strange occurrences throughout the park.

The official explanation is simple.

He retired.

The evidence suggests something else happened.

---

# The Missing Ranger

## Elias Mercer (Placeholder Name)

Elias Mercer was a park ranger who served Blackwood Valley for decades.

He was known as:
- Dedicated
- Knowledgeable
- Protective of the park

Later in his career, he began reporting unusual incidents.

Examples:
- Impossible animal behavior
- Strange sounds
- Missing hikers
- Unexplained tracks
- Strange lights
- Equipment failures

His reports became increasingly unusual.

Eventually, others began questioning his reliability.

---

# The Disappearance

During a routine patrol, Elias disappeared.

Evidence recovered:
- Ranger vehicle
- Equipment
- Radio
- Partial journal
- Signs of a struggle

No body was found.

The investigation was eventually closed.

---

# Player Connection

The player takes over Elias' former position.

At first, the player believes Elias was mistaken.

Over time, evidence suggests he was documenting something real.

---

# Ranger Journals

Throughout the park, players discover Elias' journals.

The journals contain:
- Park history
- Survival knowledge
- Personal observations
- Warnings
- Investigations

They reveal his gradual change from skepticism to belief.

---

# Journal Progression

Early journals:
Focus on normal ranger work.

Examples:
- Wildlife notes
- Trail maintenance
- Visitor reports

---

Middle journals:
Unusual events appear.

Examples:
- Strange footprints
- Missing animals
- Unknown sounds

---

Late journals:
The mystery deepens.

Examples:
- Warnings
- Hidden locations
- Unexplained discoveries

---

# The Park Mystery

The park contains unexplained phenomena.

The game should never fully explain everything.

Possible explanations:
- Unknown wildlife
- Cryptids
- Ancient legends
- Supernatural forces
- Something else

The truth remains uncertain.

---

# Cryptids and Phenomena

Cryptids are not traditional enemies.

They are part of the park ecosystem.

Examples:

## Bigfoot

Behavior:
- Avoids humans
- Protects territory
- Leaves evidence

---

## Strange Lights

Possible causes:
- Unknown technology
- Natural phenomena
- Something beyond understanding

---

## Unknown Creatures

Evidence appears before encounters.

Examples:
- Tracks
- Sounds
- Missing equipment
- Wildlife reactions

---

# Storytelling Style

The story is discovered through:
- Journals
- Radio transmissions
- Environmental clues
- Abandoned locations
- Player experiences

The player creates the story through exploration.

---

# Design Rule

The player should always know something is wrong.

They should rarely know exactly what it is.
