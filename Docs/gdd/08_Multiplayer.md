# Multiplayer Design

## Overview

Project Blackwood is designed as a cooperative first-person experience for 1-4 players.

Players work together as park rangers managing a living wilderness.

Multiplayer should enhance teamwork, communication, and emergent moments.

---

# Player Count

Supported:
- Solo
- 2 players
- 3 players
- 4 players

The game should remain enjoyable regardless of player count.

---

# Cooperative Philosophy

Players should succeed by working together.

The goal is not competition.

The goal is:
"Protect the park together."

---

# Ranger Roles

Players are not locked into classes.

Instead, they naturally specialize through equipment and play style.

---

## Maintenance Ranger

Focus:
- Repairs
- Construction
- Equipment

Tools:
- Hammer
- Chainsaw
- Repair kits
- Building tools

---

## Wildlife Ranger

Focus:
- Animals
- Ecosystems
- Tracking

Tools:
- Tracking equipment
- Cameras
- Wildlife tools
- Medical supplies

---

## Search & Rescue Ranger

Focus:
- Emergencies
- Missing visitors
- Medical response

Tools:
- Rescue equipment
- Navigation tools
- Emergency supplies

---

## Fire Ranger

Focus:
- Fire response
- Environmental hazards

Tools:
- Fire equipment
- Protective gear
- Detection tools

---

# Communication

Communication is important.

Players use:
- Voice chat
- Radios
- Signals

Radios should create immersion.

---

# Shared Progression

The park belongs to the team.

Shared progression:
- Ranger station upgrades
- Park improvements
- Unlocked regions
- Major discoveries

---

# Player Inventory

Players have personal equipment.

Some items may require teamwork.

Examples:

Heavy equipment:
- Two-person carry
- Vehicle transport
- Multiple players required

---

# Downed State

Players should not instantly die.

When injured:
Players enter a downed state.

Teammates can:
- Rescue
- Stabilize
- Carry
- Call emergency support

---

# Multiplayer Events

Events should encourage teamwork.

Examples:

## Storm

One player repairs damage.

Another guides stranded visitors.

Another monitors communications.

---

## Search Mission

Players split up:
One tracks.
One monitors radio.
One manages supplies.

---

## Strange Encounter

Players must decide:
Investigate?
Observe?
Leave?
Protect others?

---

# Design Rule

The best multiplayer moments should happen naturally.

Players should create stories together.
