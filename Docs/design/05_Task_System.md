# Task System

## Overview

Tasks are the primary gameplay activities in Project Blackwood.

They represent the daily responsibilities of a park ranger.

Tasks provide:
- Player progression
- Park improvement
- Income
- Exploration opportunities
- Opportunities for unexpected events

Tasks should feel meaningful and connected to maintaining the park.

---

# Task Categories

## Maintenance Tasks

Routine work that keeps the park operational.

Examples:
- Repair damaged trail signs
- Replace broken fences
- Fix bridges
- Clear fallen trees
- Repair cabins
- Maintain equipment

Purpose:
Shows the player their impact on the park.

---

# Environmental Tasks

Activities focused on protecting the ecosystem.

Examples:
- Remove trash
- Restore damaged habitats
- Monitor water quality
- Plant trees
- Protect nesting areas

Purpose:
Reinforces the idea that the player is protecting the park.

---

# Wildlife Tasks

Managing animal activity.

Examples:
- Track animals
- Place wildlife cameras
- Rescue injured animals
- Relocate dangerous wildlife
- Investigate animal behavior

Purpose:
Creates interaction with the living ecosystem.

---

# Visitor Tasks

Helping people in the park.

Examples:
- Give directions
- Assist campers
- Respond to complaints
- Find lost visitors
- Handle emergencies

Purpose:
Makes the park feel populated and alive.

---

# Emergency Tasks

High priority situations.

Examples:
- Wildfires
- Storm damage
- Missing hikers
- Vehicle accidents
- Medical emergencies

Purpose:
Creates tension and teamwork.

---

# Investigation Tasks

Unusual events that require investigation.

Examples:
- Strange tracks
- Missing equipment
- Unexplained sounds
- Radio signals
- Unknown sightings

Purpose:
Connects normal ranger work to mystery systems.

---

# Task Structure

Each task contains:

## Location

Where the task occurs.

---

## Objective

What needs to be completed.

---

## Required Equipment

Tools needed.

---

## Reward

Includes:
- Money
- Reputation
- Park improvement
- New discoveries

---

# Task Generation

Tasks should be generated dynamically.

The park considers:
- Weather
- Time
- Season
- Park condition
- Player progress
- Previous events

---

# Task Difficulty

Tasks scale based on:
- Distance
- Equipment requirements
- Environmental hazards
- Time pressure
- Player count

---

# Example Task

## Broken Trail Bridge

Location:
Pine Ridge Trail

Objective:
Repair damaged bridge.

Required:
- Hammer
- Replacement materials

Possible events:

Normal:
Complete repair.

Unusual:
Find strange markings nearby.

Rare:
Discover evidence from the missing ranger.

---

# Design Rule

Tasks should never feel like chores.

Every task should either:
- Improve the park
- Reveal something interesting
- Create a memorable player moment
